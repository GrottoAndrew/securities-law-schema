## Accredited Investor Standards

### SEC Release: Accredited Investor Definition Update (2020)

**Citation**: Securities Act Release No. 33-10824 (August 26, 2020)
**Link**: [https://www.sec.gov/rules/final/2020/33-10824.pdf](https://www.sec.gov/rules/final/2020/33-10824.pdf)
**Summary**: Added new categories including professional certifications (Series 7, 65, 82), knowledgeable employees of private funds, family offices with $5M+ AUM, and "spousal equivalent" concept.

### SEC Release: Further Accredited Investor Amendments (2025)

**Citation**: Securities Act Release No. 33-11293 (February 18, 2025)
**Federal Register**: 90 FR 9687
**Link**: Search SEC.gov for Release 33-11293
**Summary**: Further updates to accredited investor definition. Verify current text at eCFR.

### E.F. Hutton & Co. (December 3, 1985)

**Topic**: Determining accredited investor status
**Summary**: Staff guidance on reasonable belief standard for investor qualification.
**Research**: Search SEC no-action letter database for "E.F. Hutton 1985"

## General Solicitation and Advertising

### IPONET (July 26, 1996)

**Topic**: Internet communications and general solicitation
**Link**: Search SEC no-action letters for "IPONET 1996"
**Summary**: Early guidance on whether internet posting constitutes general solicitation. Established that password-protected websites with qualification screening may avoid general solicitation characterization.

### Lamp Technologies, Inc. (May 29, 1997)

**Topic**: Online offering communications
**Link**: Search SEC no-action letters for "Lamp Technologies 1997"
**Summary**: Staff guidance on internet-based capital raising platforms and general solicitation concerns.

### SEC Release: Rule 506(c) Adoption (2013)

**Citation**: Securities Act Release No. 33-9415 (July 10, 2013)
**Link**: [https://www.sec.gov/rules/final/2013/33-9415.pdf](https://www.sec.gov/rules/final/2013/33-9415.pdf)
**Summary**: Adopted Rule 506(c) permitting general solicitation if all purchasers are verified accredited investors. Implements JOBS Act Section 201(a).

### SEC Guidance: General Solicitation in Rule 506 Offerings (2017)

**Citation**: C&DI Questions 256.23-256.35
**Link**: [https://www.sec.gov/divisions/corpfin/guidance/securitiesactrules-interps.htm](https://www.sec.gov/divisions/corpfin/guidance/securitiesactrules-interps.htm)
**Summary**: Staff interpretations on demo day presentations, pre-existing relationships, and what constitutes general solicitation.

## Integration Doctrine

### SEC Release: Integration Framework Update (2020)

**Citation**: Securities Act Release No. 33-10884 (November 2, 2020)
**Link**: [https://www.sec.gov/rules/final/2020/33-10884.pdf](https://www.sec.gov/rules/final/2020/33-10884.pdf)
**Summary**: Modernized integration safe harbors under Rule 152. Establishes general principle that offers 30+ days apart are not integrated.

### Black Box, Inc. (June 26, 1990)

**Topic**: Integration of concurrent offerings
**Summary**: Classic no-action letter on integration analysis factors.
**Research**: Search SEC no-action letters for "Black Box 1990"

### Squadron, Ellenoff, Plesent & Lehrer (February 28, 1992)

**Topic**: Integration with employee offerings
**Summary**: Guidance on whether employee stock plans integrate with Regulation D offerings.
**Research**: Search SEC no-action letters for "Squadron Ellenoff 1992"

## How to Research Additional Letters

### SEC No-Action Letter Database

The SEC maintains a searchable database of no-action letters:

**Primary Search**: [https://www.sec.gov/cgi-bin/browse-edgar?action=getcompany&type=no-act](https://www.sec.gov/cgi-bin/browse-edgar?action=getcompany&type=no-act)

**Alternative**: [https://www.sec.gov/divisions/corpfin/cf-noaction.shtml](https://www.sec.gov/divisions/corpfin/cf-noaction.shtml)

### Search Strategies

1. **By Topic**: Use keywords like "Regulation D", "Rule 506", "accredited investor", "general solicitation"
2. **By Date**: Filter to relevant time periods
3. **By Company**: Search for companies known to have received guidance
4. **By Staff Division**: Filter to Division of Corporation Finance

### Third-Party Resources

Commercial databases with compiled no-action letters:

- Westlaw: SEC No-Action Letters database
- LexisNexis: Federal Securities library
- Bloomberg Law: Securities Practice Center

**Note**: These are subscription services. The SEC's own database is free but less searchable.
